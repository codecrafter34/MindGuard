=== WHAT IS MENTAL HEALTH? ===
Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood.

Mental health problems are common. In any given year, one in five adults experiences a mental illness. Mental health problems are not a sign of weakness. They are real health conditions, just like heart disease or diabetes.

=== DEPRESSION ===
Depression (major depressive disorder) is a common and serious medical illness that negatively affects how you feel, the way you think, and how you act. It is not simply feeling sad for a few days. Symptoms must last at least two weeks and represent a change from previous functioning.

Common symptoms of depression include:
- Feeling sad or experiencing a depressed mood
- Loss of interest or pleasure in activities once enjoyed
- Changes in appetite — weight loss or gain
- Trouble sleeping or sleeping too much
- Loss of energy or increased fatigue
- Increase in purposeless physical activity or slowed movements
- Feeling worthless or guilty
- Difficulty thinking, concentrating, or making decisions
- Thoughts of death or suicide

Depression is among the most treatable of mental disorders. Between 80% and 90% of people with depression eventually respond well to treatment. Treatment options include medication, psychotherapy, or a combination.

If you experience symptoms of depression, please speak with a qualified mental health professional. MindGuard AI does not diagnose.

=== ANXIETY ===
Anxiety disorders are the most common mental health condition. They involve more than temporary worry or fear. For a person with an anxiety disorder, the anxiety does not go away and can get worse over time.

Types of anxiety disorders include:
- Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD): excessive worry about many things
- Social Anxiety Disorder: intense fear of social situations
- Panic Disorder: recurrent, unexpected panic attacks
- Phobias: intense fear of specific things or situations

Physical symptoms of anxiety may include racing heart, sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, dizziness, and stomach problems.

Anxiety disorders are highly treatable. Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) and medication are among the most effective treatments.

=== STRESS AND COPING ===
Stress is a normal reaction to challenging situations. Short-term stress can be helpful — it motivates us to act. But chronic stress, when it persists over weeks or months, can harm mental and physical health.

Healthy coping strategies include:
- Regular physical exercise (even 30 minutes of walking)
- Maintaining a consistent sleep routine (7–9 hours for adults)
- Deep breathing exercises and relaxation techniques
- Talking to a trusted friend, family member, or counsellor
- Limiting caffeine and alcohol
- Taking breaks and practicing mindfulness
- Setting realistic goals and limits

=== MINDFULNESS AND MEDITATION ===
Mindfulness means paying attention to the present moment with curiosity and without judgment. Research shows mindfulness practice can reduce symptoms of anxiety and depression and improve overall well-being.

A simple mindfulness exercise:
1. Find a comfortable seated position.
2. Close your eyes or soften your gaze.
3. Focus on your breath — the sensation of inhaling and exhaling.
4. When your mind wanders (it will), gently return attention to the breath.
5. Start with 5 minutes and build gradually.

Apps like Headspace and Insight Timer offer free guided meditations. These are tools, not medical treatment.

=== SLEEP AND MENTAL HEALTH ===
Sleep and mental health are closely connected. Poor sleep worsens anxiety, depression, and cognitive function. Improving sleep habits (sleep hygiene) can significantly improve mental health.

Sleep hygiene tips:
- Go to bed and wake at the same time each day, including weekends
- Keep the bedroom dark, cool, and quiet
- Avoid screens (phone, TV, computer) for at least one hour before bed
- Avoid large meals, caffeine, and alcohol close to bedtime
- Do not lie in bed awake for more than 20 minutes — get up and do something calm

=== WHEN TO SEEK PROFESSIONAL HELP ===
You should consider speaking with a mental health professional if:
- Symptoms interfere with daily life, work, or relationships for more than two weeks
- You are having thoughts of harming yourself or others
- You feel unable to cope with day-to-day challenges
- You are using substances to cope
- You feel persistently hopeless or empty

A mental health professional can provide an accurate diagnosis and appropriate treatment. MindGuard AI is not a substitute for professional care.

=== ABOUT THERAPY ===
Psychotherapy (talk therapy) is an effective treatment for many mental health conditions. Common evidence-based approaches include:

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT): Helps identify and change unhelpful thought patterns and behaviours. Effective for depression, anxiety, PTSD, OCD.

Dialectical Behaviour Therapy (DBT): Combines CBT with mindfulness. Particularly effective for emotional regulation difficulties.

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT): Encourages acceptance of difficult thoughts and commitment to values-based action.

Interpersonal Therapy (IPT): Focuses on improving interpersonal relationships and communication.
